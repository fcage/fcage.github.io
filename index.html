who is this?

Feilding Cage, a developer, designer and journalist, builds interactive stories that have captured the attention and imagination of a global audience. A grad of UNC's school of journalism and NYU's graphic communication technolgoy and management program, his entrepreneurial interests include product development and, currently, sensors.

Feilding works mostly with javascript, html, css, d3, jquery, underscore, grunt and ruby. He has some experience with node, php and python.

He loves to run because he loves to eat. You'll probably find Feilding in the dessert line.

Read more

As an interactive journalist for The Guardian, highlights of Feilding's work include live election results (2012), single-serve sites, and visualizing data with social and personalization hooks.

From 2009-2011 as the supervising interactive developer at the Associated Press, he created templates and workflows that allow AP's more than 35 designers and developers to publish interactive content in an agile manner as news breaks. This standardization more than doubled the output of AP's digital content and led to early iterations of next-generation html-only content.

Previously as an interactive designer at TIME, Feilding was involved in the full product lifecycle from capturing goals, designing prototypes, user testing, writing documentation for developers and post-launch enhancements. His work included the video section, article page, several consumer marketing sites and a site allowing readers to upload their photo to TIME's person of the year billboard in Times Square.
